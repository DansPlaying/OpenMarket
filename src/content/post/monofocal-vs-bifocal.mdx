## What are Monofocal Lenses?  

First, let’s start by defining what **monofocal** means. The term explains itself:  
- “Mono” means “one.”  
- “Focal” refers to “focus” or “vision.”  

These lenses are characterized by rectifying vision defects in one type of focus.  

In this sense, single vision glasses work to see **near** or **far**, but they are not suitable for improving vision in cases where both distances are problematic.  

Monofocal lenses have the same formulation across their entire surface. Therefore, they address only **one condition** at a time. If a patient begins to have trouble seeing at both distances, they will likely need **two pairs** of monofocal lenses with different formulas:  

- One pair to see up close.  
- Another pair to see far away.  

However, there is an additional option we’ll explore in this comparison: **progressive lenses**.  

---

## What are Progressive Lenses?  

Progressive lenses, unlike monofocal lenses, offer an **integrated formulation**. They are designed to allow the eye to focus on **three distances**:  
- **Far**  
- **Intermediate**  
- **Near**  

### Design Features of Progressive Lenses:  
- **Upper part of the lens:** Intended for distant vision.  
- **Middle part of the lens:** Optimized for intermediate distances.  
- **Lower part of the lens:** Designed for near vision.  

With progressive lenses, users can perform a variety of tasks **without switching glasses**, making them versatile and convenient.  

### Appearance and Adaptation:  
Progressive lenses look similar to monofocal lenses as they don’t have visible lines or segments to demarcate different focus areas. However, they require a short adaptation period for most users.  

**Note:** Progressive lenses may have marginal areas on the sides without a prescription, so users need time to learn how to use them effectively.  

---

## What Type of Lenses Is Best for Each User?  

To compare **monofocal lenses** and **progressive lenses**, it’s important to understand their intended use cases.  

### Monofocal Lenses  
Recommended for individuals with these characteristics:  
- Problems seeing **near** or **far**.  
- Conditions like **myopia**, **hyperopia**, and **astigmatism**.  
- Typically prescribed for users **under 40 years of age**.  

### Progressive Lenses  
Recommended for users with the following qualities:  
- Difficulty seeing at **both near and far distances**.  
- Conditions like **presbyopia**, often combined with **myopia**, **hyperopia**, or **astigmatism**.  
- Typically prescribed for users **over 40 years of age**.  

Progressive glasses may also be suitable for younger patients with severe visual dysfunctions affecting both near and far vision. In such cases, multifocal lenses help address multiple types of vision disorders simultaneously.  

---

By understanding the differences and purposes of these lenses, you can make an informed decision about which option best suits your vision needs.
